--- layout: post title: UC Berkeley civic-mindedness breakdown date: '2010-10-11T23:07:00.001-07:00' author: garymm tags: modified_time: '2010-10-11T23:13:22.324-07:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1383463705740489547.post-8451709716295425098 --- Here are some details I thought were bogging down my post on the civic-mindedness of UC Berkeley students.

My source is UC Berkeley's UCUES results for 2008 and 2009.
You can see my spreadsheet analyzing the civic engagement numbers.

Here are the responses to the question "In the presidential election this November, will you vote?":
 
Here's a numerical representation of the chart above:
Definitely will: 0.655
Probably will:  0.176
Maybe: 0.093
Will not*: 0.075

*Will not = (probably not + definitely not) - non-citizens

I have subtracted out non-citizens from these numbers to try to give a better idea of who's actually choosing to vote.

The conclusion: Combining those who say "Definitely" and "Probably", more than 8 out of 10 UC Berkeley students planned on voting in 2008.

2008 was a presidential election year that generated a lot of excitement. Let's look at the results from 2009. This time the question was "When you next have the opportunity to vote, will you?":






Again, the numbers:
Definitely will: 0.610
Probably will:  0.275
Maybe: 0.086
Will not: 0.029

The conclusion: In 2009, nearly 9 out of 10 students planned on voting.


I think it's also instructive to look at the responses to the question "In the most recent election season, to what extent did you do talk about the campaign with other students?"



Let's say that a student is actively discussing politics if he or she answered "A great deal" or "A significant amount". The proportion of students actively discussing politics was:
2008: 0.35
2009: 0.46

Bear with me as I introduce one more survey question: "In the most recent election season, to what extent did you pay attention to candidates and issue?"





Now the proportion of students actively paying attention to the issues:
2008: 0.43
2009: 0.51

Note that these figures on discussing and paying attention to the campaign include non-citizens, while those on voting do not.
